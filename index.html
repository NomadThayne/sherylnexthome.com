About the Town

A place like no other

Ojai — pronounced "OH-hi" — takes its name from the Chumash word for "moon." The valley sits in a rare east-west running mountain range, which creates the legendary "Pink Moment": at sunset, the Topatopa Mountains turn a luminous shade of pink while the valley below falls into shadow.

The town has attracted artists, writers, and seekers for over a century — from philosopher Krishnamurti, who spent much of his life here, to the generations of potters, painters, and musicians who still form the backbone of its community today.

Ojai's agriculture shapes daily life: avocados, citrus, honey, and wine grapes thrive in the valley soil. The Sunday market is a town institution. Despite its proximity to Los Angeles, Ojai has maintained strict development limits — preserving its small-town character and the agricultural land that defines the valley.
